Jimi Hendrix

The man who set fire on a guitar

Jimi's Timeline

  • 1942 - Johnny Allen Hendrix was born in November, 1942. He was the oldest of his parents' five children. His mother was only 17.
  • 1946 - Following his father's honorable discharge from the army, Johnny's name was changed to James Marshall Hendrix by his father.
  • 1951 - Hendrix's parents divorced. His father received primary custody of the children, and raised Jimi with the help of his paternal grandmother.
  • 1957 - Jimi's interest in music was growing. He saw Elvis Presley perform at Sicks Stadium that year.
  • 1958 - Jimi's father bought him his first acoustic guitar, used, from a friend for $5.00. By the same year, when Jimi was 15, his mother, an alcoholic, died of liver cirrhosis. While he had been raised by his father, Jimi had idolized his mother.
  • 1959 - Jimi's father bought him his first electric guitar in 1959, and with friends, Jimi began playing small gigs locally.
  • 1961 - Following some trouble with the law for riding in stolen cars, Jimi was offered the choice of prison or the army. He chose the army, and volunteered as a paratrooper. He was injured in a jump and honorably discharged the following year.
  • 1962 - Between 1962 and 1965, Hendrix worked as a gig and studio musician, working closely with a number of well-known rhythm and blues musicians.
  • 1966 - Jimi founded Jimmy James and the Blue Flames. The band was short lived, and marked the last time the spelling Jimmy was used. In London, Hendrix formed the Jimi Hendrix Experience. The band would gain rapid success, with chart topping songs by February 1967.
  • 1969 - Following a successful American tour, at the height of its popularity, the Jimi Hendrix Experience broke up. Jimi Hendrix played at Woodstock, debuting a new band, Gypsy Sun and Rainbows made up of old friends.
  • 1970 - After initially debuting as Gypsy Sun and Rainbows, by 1970, Band of Gypsies had formed. Band of Gypsies consisted of Henrix, Billy Cox and Buddy Miles. The group began a tour in 1970.
  • 1970 - On September 18, 1970, Hendrix was found dead in his hotel room in London following an overdose of sleeping pills.
